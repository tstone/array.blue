Focused Conversation Method/Framework

Presented by Titus Stone

Do these situations sound familiar?

- You're in a meeting where there is a lot of talking but things are going nowhere.

- You're in a meeting where people are arguing over a solution because neither of them have all the facts or don't agree on what the facts are.

The focused conversation method...

1.) orients things in the right order and

2.) allocates time for each step of a conversation to make sure a result is achieved.

Every focused conversation has 4 parts

Observation - What are the facts?

Response - How do we feel about the facts?

Interpretation - What conclusions can we draw from the above two?

Decision - What changes or applications can we draw from the above three?

Another way to think of this is data gathering and action

Observation + Response = generating data about the topoic and ourselves.

Interpretation + Descision = judging the facts and doing something about them.

Each segment O-R-I-D is timeboxed

Facilitating a focused conversation means taking the time you have, then dividing it by 4

Example: 30 minute meeting = 7.5 minutes for observation, 7.5 minutes for response, etc.

Prepare for a focused conversation by preparing questions for participants

Focused conversations aren't one person talking about what should be done, but one person asking the group questions and discovering solutions together
